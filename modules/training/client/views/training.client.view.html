Geko Instructions

1. Identify the Osteophyte Region

Mark the edges of the osteophyte (green in image above). If no osteophyte,
control-click to skip.

2. Identify the Lesion Surface Points

Mark the cartilage surface that has been lost or fibrilated due to osteoarthritis
(pink in image above). This line must be parallel to the cartilage surface, defining the
surface layer of the cartilage. Depth of lesion is defined as the perpendicular distance
from this line.

3. Identify the Lesion Border

Mark the surface of the cartilage lesion (grey in image above). If no lesion,
control-click to skip.

4. Identify the Plateau Span

Mark the edges of the tibial plateau (orange in image above),
measuring the tibial plateau width.

5. Identify the Osteochondral Interface

Define the osteochondral interface (dark green in image above).

6. Identify the Cartilage Surface

Define the cartilage surface (light orange in image above).
